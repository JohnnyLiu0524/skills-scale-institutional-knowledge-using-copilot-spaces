## OctoAcme Project Management Principles

Our project management approach is built on five core principles:

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in every decision
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments rather than large monolithic releases
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has a named Project Manager and Product Lead with defined responsibilities
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence and metrics
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, learning, and continuous improvement without fear

## Project Lifecycle

Every OctoAcme project follows this standardized five-phase lifecycle:

1. **Initiation** — Validate the business need, identify stakeholders, and create a high-level timeline
2. **Planning** — Define scope, allocate resources, identify milestones and dependencies, and establish the roadmap
3. **Execution** — Build, test, review, and iterate based on feedback and acceptance criteria
4. **Release** — Deploy to production, verify functionality, and communicate to stakeholders
5. **Close & Retrospective** — Capture learnings, document improvements, and archive project artifacts

## Core Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Project Manager** | Coordinates delivery, manages schedules, risks, and communications; ensures transparency and alignment |
| **Product Manager** | Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, measures success, and validates solutions |
| **Developers** | Implement features, collaborate on design and testability, participate in reviews and planning |
| **QA/Testing** | Validate quality, verify acceptance criteria, and ensure reliability before release |
| **Stakeholders** | Provide inputs, approvals, and strategic direction for projects |

## Key Artifacts

Projects use these key artifacts for planning and tracking:

- **Project Charter / One-pager** — Business case, goals, success metrics, timeline
- **Roadmap and Release Plan** — Milestones, releases, and feature prioritization
- **Sprint/Iteration Backlog** — Prioritized work items with acceptance criteria
- **Acceptance Criteria & Definition of Done** — Quality standards and completion requirements
- **Risk Register** — Identified risks with impact, probability, and mitigation plans
- **Retrospective Notes** — Learnings and action items for continuous improvement

## Communication Cadence

Consistent communication across the project team and stakeholders:

- **Weekly sync** — PM and Product Manager alignment on progress and decisions
- **Twice-weekly standups** — Delivery team syncs on progress, blockers, and dependencies (or as agreed)
- **Monthly stakeholder updates** — High-level progress and risk summaries for sponsors and stakeholders
- **Ad-hoc escalations** — Urgent blockers and issues raised immediately using escalation paths
